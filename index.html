Key Specs and What they mean:

Pin height:

Pin height is fairly self-explanatory, it's just the height of each key pin. Changing the pin height is how you change which key works. Some manufacturers start at pin 0, some start at pin 1.

Bitting depth:

Bitting depth is tricky, it's name implies that you are measuring the depth of the cut, but it really measures the height from the bottom of the key to the top of the cut, a smaller bitting depth value is a deeper cut.

Cut Spacing:

Cut spacing is the distance from the shoulder of the key to the center of a cut. NOTE: on BEST key systems, the cuts are measured from the tip, not the shoulder.

MACS:

MACS or Maximum Adjacent Cut Specification refers to the maximum difference allowed between one pinned chamber and its adjacent pinned chamber.
